# PawPal+ Feeding Guidelines

## Dogs

### Daily Requirements
- **Adult dogs**: Feed 1-2 times daily, typically morning and evening
- **Puppies (under 6 months)**: Feed 3-4 times daily to support growth
- **Senior dogs (7+ years)**: Feed 2 times daily with smaller portions
- **Portion size**: Generally 2-3% of body weight per day, split across meals

### Water
- Fresh water available at all times
- Dogs need approximately 1 ounce per pound of body weight daily
- Increase water intake during hot weather or exercise

### Feeding Schedules
- Consistent feeding times help regulate digestion and house training
- Morning: 6-8 AM typical
- Evening: 6-8 PM typical
- Space meals at least 6-8 hours apart

### Food Types
- Dry kibble: Store in airtight containers, discard after 4 weeks of opening
- Wet food: Cover and refrigerate after opening, use within 2-3 days
- Raw diets: Consult veterinarian, risk of foodborne illness
- Human food: Avoid chocolate, grapes, onions, xylitol, avocado

## Cats

### Daily Requirements
- **Adult cats**: Feed 1-2 times daily with 24-30 calories per pound of body weight
- **Kittens (under 1 year)**: Feed 3-4 times daily
- **Senior cats (10+ years)**: Feed 2 times daily, monitor weight changes
- **Portion size**: Typically 3.5-4.5 ounces per meal for average adult cat

### Water
- Cats drink less water than dogs but need consistent access
- Offer water bowls in multiple locations
- Some cats prefer running water (consider a cat fountain)
- Average intake: 3.5-4.5 mL per pound of body weight daily

### Feeding Schedules
- Morning: 6-7 AM
- Evening: 6-7 PM
- Cats have smaller stomachs; smaller meals more frequently is better

### Food Types
- Wet food: Cover and refrigerate, use within 2-3 days of opening
- Dry food: Store in cool, dry place in airtight container
- Kittens require higher protein and fat content
- Avoid onions, garlic, chocolate, and raw dough

## Special Dietary Needs

### Medical Conditions
- **Diabetes**: Prescription diets available, feed on regular schedule before insulin
- **Kidney disease**: Low protein prescription diets, specific portion control needed
- **Obesity**: Reduce portion sizes gradually, increase activity
- **Digestive issues**: Bland diet (chicken and rice) may be recommended, consult vet

### Allergies and Sensitivities
- Common allergens: Chicken, beef, dairy, wheat
- Elimination diets take 8-12 weeks to see improvement
- Keep detailed food logs to identify triggers
- Never change diet abruptly; mix new food gradually over 7-10 days

### Supplements and Treats
- Treats should be no more than 10% of daily calories
- Maintain weight by reducing main meal portions when giving treats
- Supplements like fish oil or joint support need veterinary approval
