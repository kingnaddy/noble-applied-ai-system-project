# PawPal+ Grooming Guidelines

## Dogs

### Bathing
- **Frequency**: Every 4-12 weeks depending on coat type and activity level
- **Short-haired dogs**: Every 8-12 weeks
- **Medium-haired dogs**: Every 6-8 weeks
- **Long-haired dogs**: Every 4-6 weeks or more if needed
- **Water temperature**: Lukewarm (85-92°F), never hot
- **Duration**: 5-15 minutes, longer can dry out skin
- **Drying**: Use soft towel, avoid heat dryers on high setting

### Brushing
- **Short-haired breeds**: 2-3 times per week with slicker brush
- **Medium-haired breeds**: 3-4 times per week
- **Long-haired breeds**: Daily brushing to prevent mats
- **Best time**: Brush before bathing to remove loose fur
- **Shedding seasons**: Increase frequency during spring and fall

### Nail Care
- **Frequency**: Every 3-4 weeks or when nails touch the floor
- **Length**: Should not touch ground when standing
- **Trimming**: Cut at 45-degree angle, avoid the pink quick
- **Signs of overgrowth**: Clicking on floors, walking difficulty
- **Professional help**: If you're unsure about quick location

### Ear Cleaning
- **Frequency**: Weekly for floppy-eared breeds, monthly for others
- **Signs of problems**: Bad odor, discharge, scratching
- **Process**: Use ear cleaning solution, gently flush with syringe
- **Do not**: Use cotton swabs deep in canal (risk of eardrum damage)

### Dental Care
- **Brushing**: Ideally daily, minimum 3-4 times per week
- **Tooth paste**: Use pet-specific, never human toothpaste
- **Frequency of professional cleaning**: Annual check-up, may need professional cleaning every 1-2 years
- **Signs of dental problems**: Bad breath, tooth loss, drooling

## Cats

### Bathing
- **Frequency**: Rarely needed, only 1-2 times per year for most cats
- **When needed**: Outdoor cats, long-haired cats in hot weather, skin conditions
- **Water depth**: Fill tub with 1-2 inches of lukewarm water
- **Drying**: Cats groom themselves; provide towel and warm, quiet place
- **Note**: Most cats groom themselves effectively

### Brushing
- **Short-haired cats**: 2-3 times per week
- **Long-haired cats**: Daily brushing to prevent mats and hairballs
- **Matted fur**: Gently work out with slicker brush or clipper
- **Shedding**: Increase frequency during shedding season (spring/fall)
- **Benefits**: Reduces hairballs, improves coat health, bonding time

### Nail Care
- **Frequency**: Every 2-3 weeks
- **Length**: Trim only the clear part, avoid pink quick
- **Scratching post**: Provide scratchers to help cats maintain nails naturally
- **Signs of problems**: Getting caught on fabric, limping

### Ear Cleaning
- **Frequency**: Monthly check, clean only if waxy buildup present
- **Signs of problems**: Head shaking, scratching, redness, discharge
- **Cleaning**: Use damp cotton ball, gently wipe visible area only
- **Do not**: Insert cleaning solutions or swabs into ear canal

### Dental Care
- **Brushing**: Ideally 3-4 times per week (cats resist daily more than dogs)
- **Toothpaste**: Use feline-specific enzymatic toothpaste
- **Frequency of professional cleaning**: Annual vet check, professional cleaning every 1-2 years if needed
- **Signs of dental problems**: Bad breath, difficulty eating, tooth discoloration

## Special Grooming Needs

### Mats and Tangles
- **Prevention**: Regular brushing is the best prevention
- **Removal**: Use mat splitter or slicker brush, work gently from outside
- **Severe matting**: May require professional grooming or clipping
- **Note**: Matted fur can harbor parasites and cause skin irritation

### Skin Conditions
- **Dry skin**: Use moisturizing shampoo, add omega-3 supplements
- **Itching**: May indicate allergies, fleas, or infections; consult vet
- **Yeast or fungal**: Medicated shampoo prescribed by veterinarian
- **Bathing frequency**: May increase if treating condition, consult vet

### Anal Gland Expression
- **Signs of problems**: Scooting, licking rear, discomfort
- **Manual expression**: Many pet owners learn this at home; can be done by groomer
- **Frequency**: Only when needed, not preventive
- **Veterinary option**: Vet can assess if expression is necessary

### Professional Grooming
- **When to use**: For breeds requiring hand-stripping, severe mats, or owner preference
- **Frequency**: Every 4-8 weeks depending on breed
- **Cost**: Typically $50-$150+ depending on size and services
- **Preparation**: Brush before appointment to prevent additional charges for matting
