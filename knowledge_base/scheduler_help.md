# PawPal+ Scheduler Help Guide

## Getting Started with PawPal+ Scheduling

### What is PawPal+?
PawPal+ is a smart pet care scheduler designed to help busy pet owners manage daily care routines. It creates automatic daily schedules based on your pets' needs, your availability, and task priorities. It also explains why tasks are scheduled at specific times.

### Key Concepts

#### Pets
Each pet in your household gets a profile with:
- **Name**: What you call your pet
- **Species**: Dog, cat, or other
- **Age**: How old your pet is
- **Medical Conditions**: Any health issues affecting care (diabetes, arthritis, etc.)

Your pet's medical conditions help PawPal+ prioritize essential care tasks.

#### Tasks
Tasks are the care activities your pet needs:
- **Name**: "Morning walk", "Insulin injection", "Feeding", etc.
- **Category**: walk, feeding, meds, grooming, enrichment
- **Priority**: high (medications), medium (regular exercise), low (optional enrichment)
- **Frequency**: daily, weekly, or as-needed
- **Duration**: How long the task takes (in minutes)
- **Preferred time**: morning, afternoon, or evening

#### Availability
Availability windows are the time blocks when you're free to care for your pets:
- Set on the "Owner" section
- Defined as time ranges (e.g., 7:00 AM - 9:00 AM for morning availability)
- Schedule includes multiple windows if you have gaps in your day

#### Schedule
The schedule is the final daily plan showing:
- What time each task happens
- Which pet it's for
- How long it will take
- Why PawPal+ placed it at this time

### How PawPal+ Schedules Tasks

#### 1. Task Ranking
PawPal+ ranks all due tasks by importance:
1. **Medical tasks** for pets with conditions (highest priority)
2. **High-priority tasks** like medications and feeding
3. **Medium-priority tasks** like exercise and walks
4. **Low-priority tasks** like enrichment activities

#### 2. Time Preference Matching
Once ranked, PawPal+ tries to match each task to its preferred time:
- **Morning tasks** fit in your 6 AM - 12 PM window
- **Afternoon tasks** fit in your 12 PM - 5 PM window
- **Evening tasks** fit in your 5 PM - 10 PM window

If the preferred time window is full, the task moves to the next available window.

#### 3. Window Fitting
PawPal+ places tasks in your availability windows, checking for:
- Enough time for the task duration
- No overlapping with other scheduled tasks
- Respecting task spacing (e.g., medications 12+ hours apart if daily)

#### 4. Conflict Detection
If any tasks cannot fit (even with all windows used), PawPal+ shows:
- Which tasks are unscheduled
- Why they don't fit
- How much extra time you'd need
- Suggestions for resolving conflicts

### Common Scheduling Scenarios

#### Scenario 1: Your Dog's Medication is Scheduled at Night
**Why?** High-priority medical tasks are scheduled first, and your evening window had the best fit.

**Solution options:**
- Extend your morning availability
- Reduce other morning tasks
- Ask about once-daily medication timing with your vet

#### Scenario 2: The Walk is Not Scheduled
**Why?** The schedule is full with higher-priority tasks, or no availability window matches the preferred time.

**Solution options:**
- Extend your availability windows
- Mark the walk as "as-needed" if it's flexible
- Split tasks into shorter portions
- Reduce lower-priority tasks

#### Scenario 3: Two Pets' Tasks Conflict (Same Time Window)
**Why?** Both pets need the same time window, and one has to be moved.

**Solution options:**
- Extend your availability
- Offset preferred times (one pet morning walks, one pet evening walks)
- Reduce task frequency for one pet
- Check if either task duration can be reduced

### Tips for Better Scheduling

#### 1. Set Realistic Availability
- Include all times when you can actually help with pet care
- Account for commute time, work, sleep
- Remember: 7 AM - 9 PM might be too optimistic with an 8-hour job

#### 2. Use Preferred Times Wisely
- Morning walks help dogs eliminate early in the day
- Evening feeding helps align pets with your bedtime routine
- Afternoon medication timing depends on medication type

#### 3. Prioritize Wisely
- Medical tasks and feeding should always be high-priority
- Exercise frequency depends on breed and age
- Grooming and enrichment are important but flexible

#### 4. Update Regularly
- Mark tasks complete as you finish them
- PawPal+ recalculates based on completion status
- Weekly tasks spawn new instances after completion

#### 5. Monitor Conflict Warnings
- Unscheduled tasks mean you need more time or availability
- Conflicts show which tasks to postpone or reduce
- Solutions often involve extending availability, not reducing care

### Task Categories Explained

#### Walking
- Essential for dogs, optional for cats
- Breeds vary: high-energy dogs need 1-2 hours daily
- Should happen during your availability window
- Affects house training and behavior management

#### Feeding
- Dogs: usually 1-2 times daily
- Cats: usually 1-2 times daily
- Timing helps with digestion and behavior
- Should be consistent times each day

#### Medications
- Prescribed by veterinarian
- Timing critical for effectiveness
- Should mark as high-priority
- Track completion in PawPal+

#### Grooming
- Brushing: vary by coat type and season
- Bathing: every 4-12 weeks for dogs
- Nail care: every 3-4 weeks
- Dental care: ideally daily brushing

#### Enrichment
- Play, toys, training, mental stimulation
- Prevents boredom and destructive behavior
- Often flexible frequency
- Can be combined with other activities (play during walk)

### Troubleshooting

#### Issue: "No pets yet. Add one above."
**Solution:** Add a pet first in the "Pets" section before adding tasks.

#### Issue: "Add a pet first before adding tasks."
**Solution:** Use the "Pets" section to add at least one pet.

#### Issue: Tasks keep appearing unscheduled
**Reasons:**
- Availability is too limited
- Too many high-priority tasks for available time
- Task durations are too long

**Solutions:**
- Extend availability window
- Mark lower-priority tasks as "as-needed"
- Check if task durations are realistic
- Reduce frequency of lower-priority tasks

#### Issue: The schedule doesn't look right
**Check:**
- Did you set availability for today?
- Are all pets added with their correct information?
- Are task priorities realistic?
- Can tasks fit in your availability windows?

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I schedule tasks for multiple days?**
A: PawPal+ currently schedules for one day at a time. Daily and weekly recurring tasks automatically spawn new instances after completion.

**Q: Why doesn't my task show up in the schedule?**
A: It might not fit in your availability, or it might not be due today. Check the unscheduled tasks section.

**Q: Can I manually change when a task is scheduled?**
A: PawPal+ automatically optimizes the schedule. You can mark tasks complete to update the schedule, or adjust availability windows.

**Q: What if I need tasks at specific exact times?**
A: Set your time-of-day preferences (morning/afternoon/evening), and adjust your availability windows accordingly.

**Q: Can multiple people use PawPal+?**
A: Currently, one owner profile per session. Work together on the same device, or share notes about who handles each task.
